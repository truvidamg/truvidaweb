01

The building blocks of everything

Amino acids are what your body uses to build muscle, repair tissue, produce neurotransmitters, and regulate metabolism.

Why inject instead of supplement?
  • Higher bioavailability — oral amino acids compete for gut transport
  • Therapeutic doses — impractical to eat enough protein for specific therapeutic benefits
  • Targeted timing — pre-workout carnitine or post-workout BCAAs when most useful
  • Critical during caloric deficit — shields against muscle loss on GLP-1
02

The menu

Each amino acid has a specific therapeutic role.

L-CarnitineFat burning

Transports fatty acids into mitochondria. Best pre-workout.

BCAAsMuscle

Leucine, isoleucine, valine. Stimulate muscle protein synthesis, preserve muscle during deficit.

L-GlutamineRecovery + gut

Supports immune function, gut lining integrity, and post-exercise recovery.

L-ArginineCirculation

Precursor to nitric oxide — improves blood flow. Supports libido and erectile function.

L-TaurineHeart + focus

Supports cardiac function, reduces oxidative stress, enhances focus.

GlycineSleep

Improves sleep quality, supports collagen, calms the nervous system.

L-LysineImmune

Supports immune function, collagen, calcium absorption.

GAC (Glutamine·Arginine·Carnitine)Performance stack

Muscle recovery + blood flow + fat-burning in a single injection.

Pairing with GLP-1

This is where amino acid injections become clinically important — not just performance-enhancing.

The muscle-loss problem on GLP-1

On semaglutide or tirzepatide, appetite suppression is so strong that most patients under-eat protein. 20–40% of GLP-1 weight loss can come from muscle when protein and resistance training aren't prioritized.

05

Safety notes

Expected sensations
  • Mild injection site soreness 24 hours
  • Brief warm flush immediately after
  • Arginine may cause facial flushing/headache — inject slowly
  • Glycine may cause brief drowsiness — desired for sleep protocols
  • No downtime
Caution / contraindications
  • Arginine — avoid with active herpes, recent heart attack, low blood pressure
  • BCAAs — avoid in maple syrup urine disease, advanced kidney disease
  • Lysine — caution with kidney disease
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding — discuss with your provider

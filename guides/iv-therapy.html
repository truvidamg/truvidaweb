01

Why intravenous

Oral supplements are filtered through the digestive system, where absorption can drop to 10–20%. IV therapy bypasses that entirely.

Oral supplements
  • 10–20% absorption for most vitamins
  • Degraded by stomach acid and digestive enzymes
  • Take hours to reach bloodstream
  • Absorption reduced further by poor gut health, age, or GLP-1 medications
  • Limited by how much the gut can absorb per dose
IV infusion
  • 100% bioavailability — straight into the bloodstream
  • Therapeutic levels achievable that oral cannot match
  • Effects felt within hours, not days
  • Bypasses gut absorption issues entirely
  • Custom-tuned for hydration, deficiency, or performance

03

What to expect

Your first session takes 60–75 minutes including consultation. Follow-ups run 30–45 minutes.

I

Consultation (10 min)

Medical history, current medications, goals, and recent labs are reviewed. Your provider selects or customizes a formulation.

II

Vitals + access (5 min)

Blood pressure, heart rate, and temperature are taken. A nurse places the IV — usually in the forearm or back of the hand.

III

Infusion (30–45 min)

You recline in a comfortable chair while the drip runs. Most patients read, work, or nap. Light sensations are normal — warmth, a metallic taste with B-vitamins.

IV

Post-infusion (5 min)

IV is removed, vitals rechecked, and you're released. No downtime — resume normal activity immediately.

Safety + contraindications

IV therapy is safe when administered by qualified providers.

Common sensations (normal)
  • Warmth moving up the arm as the drip starts
  • Metallic taste from B-vitamins or magnesium
  • Cool sensation at the injection site
  • Brief flushing with niacin-containing formulas
  • Urgent bathroom trip post-infusion
  • Mild bruising at IV site, resolves in 2–3 days
Requires evaluation Discuss with provider
  • Kidney disease — risk of fluid overload
  • Heart failure — fluid volume must be carefully managed
  • G6PD deficiency — high-dose vitamin C contraindicated
  • Hemochromatosis — iron-containing formulas avoided
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding — provider approval required
  • Allergy to any ingredient — alternatives available

01

What lipotropic means

Lipotropic compounds are nutrients that either break down fat, transport fat, or protect organs during fat loss. Injections deliver therapeutic doses the gut cannot absorb efficiently.

What these injections actually do
  • Mobilize fat from storage — pull triglycerides out of adipose tissue
  • Transport fat into cells — into the mitochondria where it is burned
  • Protect the liver — prevent fat accumulation during rapid weight loss
  • Boost energy metabolism — B12 and amino acid cofactors support ATP production
  • Support GLP-1 protocols — offset fatigue during semaglutide/tirzepatide titration
i

Lipo injections are not a magic fat-loss shot. They accelerate results — they do not produce them alone.

02

The three types

We use three core formulations, alone or stacked, depending on your protocol.

MIC (Methionine · Inositol · Choline)

The classic lipotropic trio. Each component plays a different role in fat metabolism:

  • Methionine — sulfur-containing amino acid, antioxidant, prevents fat buildup in liver
  • Inositol — improves insulin signaling, supports mood and cell membrane function
  • Choline — essential for transporting fat out of the liver
Typical dose: 1 mL weekly Site: glute or thigh (IM)
MIC + B12

The most common formulation we use. Combines the MIC trio with methylcobalamin (B12) for energy, mood, and neurological support.

Typical dose: 1 mL weekly Best overall combo Pairs with GLP-1
L-Carnitine

An amino acid that shuttles long-chain fatty acids into the mitochondria — literally the step where fat gets burned. Most potent on training days.

Typical dose: 1 mL 2–3× weekly Pre-workout timing Athletic performance
i

Stacking is common. Typical protocol: MIC+B12 once weekly + L-Carnitine 2× weekly on training days.

03

The mechanism in plain terms

Fat loss is a three-step process. These injections target each step.

I

Step 1 — Mobilize

Stored triglycerides in fat cells must be broken down into free fatty acids before they can be used for energy. Choline and inositol support this release.

II

Step 2 — Transport

Free fatty acids must be carried into the mitochondria — the energy factories of your cells. L-Carnitine is the carrier.

III

Step 3 — Burn

Inside the mitochondria, fatty acids are oxidized into ATP — usable cellular energy. B12 and B-complex are cofactors. Movement demands the ATP.

05

Side effects

Lipo injections are among the best-tolerated weight-loss tools we use.

Usually mild
  • Injection site soreness — 24–48 hours, mostly with MIC
  • Warm flush immediately after injection — temporary
  • Strong odor or taste — methionine sulfur content
  • Bright yellow urine — from B12 (harmless)
  • Mild stimulation — don't inject within 4 hours of bedtime
Contact provider Less common
  • Allergic reaction — rash, hives, swelling, breathing difficulty
  • Persistent redness or warmth at injection site — possible infection
  • GI upset lasting more than 24 hours
  • Palpitations — rare, but report immediately
  • Any new neurological symptom

How semaglutide works

A GLP-1 receptor agonist that mimics a natural gut hormone released after eating.

!

Off-label use: Semaglutide for weight loss is prescribed off-label at Truvida Medical Group. Your provider will monitor progress, adjust dosing, and order labs as needed. Never adjust your dose independently.

Five mechanisms of action
  • Signals fullness to the brain — reduces appetite signals in the hypothalamus, dramatically lowering hunger
  • Delays gastric emptying — food moves more slowly through your stomach, prolonging satiety
  • Reduces cravings and "food noise" — many patients report thinking far less about food
  • Lowers blood sugar — stimulates insulin release when glucose is present, suppresses glucagon
  • Supports cardiovascular health — demonstrated to reduce cardiovascular events in clinical trials

Side effects

What's normal, what's not, and when to call us immediately.

Common side effects
  • Nausea — most common, especially with dose increases. Usually improves within 2–4 weeks
  • Constipation or diarrhea — changes in GI motility are expected
  • Fatigue — especially at the start while your body adjusts
  • Reduced appetite — the desired effect. Do not let it cause dangerously low intake
  • Injection site reactions — rotate sites to minimize
  • Headache — often dehydration-related. Drink more water
Call us immediately if… Urgent
  • Severe abdominal pain radiating to the back — possible pancreatitis
  • Vision changes — sudden blurred vision or visual problems
  • Difficulty breathing or throat swelling — allergic reaction
  • Persistent vomiting — unable to keep fluids down for 24+ hours
  • Rapid heart rate at rest, unexpectedly
  • Gallbladder pain — upper-right abdominal pain after eating

Realistic month-by-month progress

Everyone responds differently, but here's a typical trajectory for patients who stay consistent.

I

Month 1 — Starting out (0.25mg)

Adjustment phase. Nausea is most common. Average loss: 1–2 kg. Focus on hydration, protein intake, and establishing habits.

II

Month 2 — Momentum (0.5–1mg)

Appetite suppression notably strengthens. Energy improving. Average 2–4 kg this month. Start a consistent exercise routine.

III

Month 3 — Progress (1–1.7mg)

Clothes fit looser. Most patients have lost 5–8 kg total. Food cravings significantly reduced.

IV

Months 4–6 — Major changes

10–15% body weight loss is common by this point. Blood pressure, cholesterol, and A1C frequently improve.

V

Month 6+ — Maintenance

Provider will guide long-term medication strategy. Lifestyle habits are now the foundation. Focus shifts to body recomposition.

05

Maximizing your results

The medication only works if your lifestyle supports it. These habits separate great responders from average ones.

  • Protein at every meal — minimum 25–30g per meal to preserve muscle
  • Resistance training 3x/week — critical for body composition. Prevents muscle loss
  • 8,000–10,000 steps/day — daily movement multiplies fat-loss results
  • Aggressive hydration — minimum 2.5–3 liters per day. Dehydration worsens every side effect
  • Eat on a schedule — even when not hungry. Set alarms. Never drop below 1,200 cal (women) or 1,500 cal (men)
  • Avoid greasy foods — they dramatically worsen nausea with semaglutide
  • Combine with Lipo/MIC injections — many patients pair their GLP-1 with weekly MIC + B12 for extra fat-burn and energy
  • Sleep 7–9 hours — poor sleep raises hunger hormones and counters the medication

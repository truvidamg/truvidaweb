Tirzepatide,
the dual agonist.

The next-generation weight-loss therapy. Tirzepatide activates two receptors — GLP-1 and GIP — producing greater appetite suppression and up to 22% body weight loss in clinical trials.

01

Why dual action matters

Tirzepatide is the first medication to activate both the GLP-1 and GIP receptors simultaneously.

!

Off-label use: Tirzepatide for weight loss is prescribed off-label at Truvida Medical Group. Your provider will monitor progress, adjust dosing, and order labs as needed.

GLP-1 + GIP — the mechanism
  • GLP-1 activation — suppresses appetite, slows gastric emptying, stimulates insulin release (same pathway as semaglutide)
  • GIP activation — enhances insulin sensitivity, improves how the body processes fat, and may reduce nausea
  • Synergy — the dual action produces greater appetite suppression and significantly more weight loss than GLP-1 alone
  • Metabolic flexibility — improves how your body switches between burning carbs and fat
  • Cardiometabolic benefits — improvements in blood pressure, lipids, and A1C frequently exceed those seen with semaglutide

03

Side effects

The side effect profile is similar to semaglutide, with some key differences.

Common side effects
  • Nausea — most common, typically worst in first 2 weeks of each new dose
  • Diarrhea — slightly more common than with semaglutide
  • Constipation — also common; increase fiber and water
  • Reduced appetite — often more pronounced than with semaglutide
  • Fatigue — common at start and with each escalation
  • Injection site reactions — rotate between abdomen, thigh, and upper arm
  • Burping/acid reflux — from slowed gastric emptying
Call us immediately if… Urgent
  • Severe abdominal pain radiating to the back — possible pancreatitis
  • Vision changes — sudden blurred or double vision
  • Throat swelling or breathing difficulty — allergic reaction
  • Persistent vomiting — unable to keep fluids down for 24+ hours
  • Low blood sugar symptoms — shaky, sweaty, confused
  • Gallbladder pain — upper-right abdominal pain after meals
  • Lump or swelling in the neck — rare but requires evaluation

Realistic month-by-month progress

Because of the slower titration, results typically start a bit later than semaglutide — but ultimately go further.

I

Month 1 — Priming (2.5mg)

Tolerance-assessment phase. Side effects most prominent. Weight loss: 1–3 kg. Focus on building injection routine and hydration.

II

Month 2 — Therapeutic (5mg)

Appetite suppression becomes pronounced. Many patients describe 'food noise' quieting. Expect 3–5 kg this month.

III

Month 3 — Acceleration (7.5mg)

Body composition visibly changing. Total loss typically 8–12 kg. Labs often start improving: A1C, blood pressure, lipids.

IV

Months 4–6 — Transformative (10–12.5mg)

Dramatic changes for most patients. 15–20% body weight loss common. Clothing sizes drop multiple times.

V

Month 6+ — Optimization

Provider decides whether to continue escalating or hold at current dose. Focus turns to maintenance, body recomposition, and muscle-building.

05

Maximizing your results

Tirzepatide is more potent than semaglutide — which means the lifestyle foundations matter even more.

  • Protein target: 1.6–2.2g/kg lean body mass — tirzepatide suppresses appetite so strongly that many patients under-eat protein.
  • Resistance training is non-negotiable — 3x per week minimum.
  • Hydration: 3–4 liters/day — higher than semaglutide recommendations because diarrhea is more common.
  • Fiber 25–35g/day — manages both diarrhea and constipation and improves satiety.
  • Eat on schedule — you will not feel hungry. Set meal alarms.
  • Avoid alcohol at dose escalations — significantly worsens nausea for 3–5 days after dose increase.
  • Combine with L-Carnitine + MIC — amplifies fat oxidation and combats the fatigue some patients experience.
  • Monitor with labs every 3 months — B12, Vitamin D, iron, lipids, A1C, and kidney function.
